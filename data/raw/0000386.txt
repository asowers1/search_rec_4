			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of songs recorded by The Rolling Stones</h1>
						<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b><a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a></b> are an English <a href="/wiki/Rock_band" title="Rock band" class="mw-redirect">rock band</a> formed in <a href="/wiki/1962_in_music" title="1962 in music">1962</a>. They have released <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones_discography" title="The Rolling Stones discography">25 studio albums</a> through 2013. The original lineup consisted of <a href="/wiki/Multi-instrumentalist" title="Multi-instrumentalist">multi-instrumentalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Brian_Jones" title="Brian Jones">Brian Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lead_vocals" title="Lead vocals" class="mw-redirect">lead vocalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Mick_Jagger" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_guitar" title="Electric guitar">guitarist</a> <a href="/wiki/Keith_Richards" title="Keith Richards">Keith Richards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitarist</a> <a href="/wiki/Bill_Wyman" title="Bill Wyman">Bill Wyman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drum_kit" title="Drum kit">drummer</a> <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Watts" title="Charlie Watts">Charlie Watts</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">keyboardist</a> <a href="/wiki/Ian_Stewart_(musician)" title="Ian Stewart (musician)">Ian Stewart</a>. Stewart was dismissed from the lineup in 1963, but continued to serve as their <a href="/wiki/Road_manager" title="Road manager">road manager</a> and de facto keyboard player. Following Jones' death in 1969, <a href="/wiki/Mick_Taylor" title="Mick Taylor">Mick Taylor</a> took over lead guitar duties until <a href="/wiki/1974_in_music" title="1974 in music">1974</a>, when he quit the group over issues of songwriting credits. He was replaced by ex <a href="/wiki/Faces_(band)" title="Faces (band)">Faces</a> guitarist <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Wood" title="Ronnie Wood">Ronnie Wood</a>. The band was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> in 1989. The group continues to write, tour, and produce to this day.</p>
