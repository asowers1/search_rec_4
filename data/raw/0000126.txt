    <h1 itemprop="name" class="ptitle">&#8216;Avatar&#8217; Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy</h1>
        <div style="margin-top:10px;"></div>
    <span class="alignleft by">
    Published 1 year ago  
    by 
    <link rel="author" href="https://plus.google.com/u/1/107168973425084682525/posts?rel=author" />

	  <p class="ggnoads" align="center"><img src="http://cdn.screenrant.com/wp-content/uploads/avatar-sequels-new-zealand.jpg" alt="avatar sequels new zealand Avatar Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy" width="620" height="320" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-402065" title="Avatar Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy" /></p>
<p>James Cameron&#8217;s vision for the future of the <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/avatar/" ><em>Avatar</em></a> franchise was originally two sequels (and maybe a prequel down the line), but that expanded to encompass an entireÂ new movie trilogy over this past year. The plan is for shooting (including, but not restricted to, motion-capture photography) to get underway by <a href="http://screenrant.com/avatar-2-3-4-filming-start-date/" >October 2014</a> &#8211; two months before Cameron <a href="http://screenrant.com/avatar-sequels-release-dates-sandy-97896/" >originally expected</a>Â <em>Avatar 2</em>Â to reach theaters, approximately one year after the first installment obliterated global box office records (for perspective).</p>
<p>The filmmaker adjusted his projected release date for <em>Avatar 2</em>Â from 2014 to 2015 over a year ago (before <a href="http://screenrant.com/avatar-2-3-4-sequels-2016-2017-2018/" >the trilogy was announced</a>). Back then, he was preparing to film (at least) part of the sequel in Wellington production studios in New Zealand &#8211; where Peter Jackson has been shooting <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/the-hobbit/" ><em>The Hobbit</em></a> trilogy over the last couple of years &#8211; not so long after Cameron had purchased a sizable portion of undeveloped real estate (read: farmland) in the country.</p>
<p>Cameron&#8217;s Lightstorm Entertainment, along with 20th Century Fox Studio and the New Zealand government, has officially confirmed that the entire new <em>Avatar</em> movie trilogy will be produced in the country, in a deal that is reportedly worth more than NZ$500 million ($412 million) and will ensure that <em>&#8220;around 90 percent&#8221;</em> of the live-action production crew on the trilogy is composed of New Zealanders.</p>

<p>Hiring will take place over the forthcoming months, as Cameron is currently overseeing the script writing for <em>Avatar 2, 3 and 4</em> from hisÂ 2,500 acres of farmland around Lake Pounui, which is a <em>&#8220;20-minute helicopter ride from the NZ capital of Wellington.&#8221;</em> (For a more thorough breakdown of the deal, read the article over at <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/new-avatar-trilogy-be-filmed-665834" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.hollywoodreporter.com']);" target="_blank"><em>THR</em></a>.)</p>
<p><img src="http://cdn.screenrant.com/wp-content/uploads/james-cameron-avatar-sequels-trilogy-570x294.jpg" alt="james cameron avatar sequels trilogy 570x294 Avatar Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy" width="570" height="294" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-402066" title="Avatar Sequels Filming in New Zealand; Cameron Talks New Trilogy" /></p>
<p>Odds are that Cameron will end up being credited as the co-screenwriter on all three <em>Avatar</em> sequels, with his scripting collaborators includingÂ Josh Friedman (<a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/the-sarah-connor-chronicles/" ><em>The Sarah Connor Chronicles</em></a>) on <em>Avatar 2</em>, Rick Jaffa and Amanda Silver (<a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/dawn-of-the-planet-of-the-apes/" ><em>Dawn of the Planet of the Apes</em></a>) on <em>Avatar 3</em> and Shane Salerno (<a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/savages/" ><em>Savages</em></a>) on <em>Avatar 4</em>.</p>
<p>In an <a href="http://www.news-sentinel.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20131216/ENT/131219716/1009/SPORTS" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.news-sentinel.com']);" target="_blank"><em>AP</em></a> interview, Cameron promised that the <em>Avatar</em> sequels will chart new territories and native inhabitants of Pandora, but revealed that the films won&#8217;t be heading off-planet (as he originally thought would be required):</p>
<blockquote><p>&#8220;It&#8217;s going to be a lot of new imagery and a lot of new environments and creatures across Pandora. We&#8217;re blowing it out all over the place. At first I thought I was going to take it onto other worlds as well, in the same solar system, but it turned out not to be necessary. I mean the Pandora that we have imagined will be a fantasy land that is going to occupy people for decades to come, the way I see it.&#8221;</p></blockquote>
<p>Of course, most would agree that it&#8217;s the stories that are the wild card factor here, not so much the expanded worldview of Pandora in these films &#8211; something thatÂ <a href="http://screenrant.com/james-cameron-writing-avatar-2-3/" >Cameron is all too aware of.</a>Â Indeed, in said <em>AP</em> interview, he also indicated the scope of the narrative in the new <em>Avatar</em> trilogy will far outstrip that in the original film:</p>
<blockquote><p>&#8220;[The first 'Avatar'] was very Jake-centric. His story seen through his eyes. We spread it around quite a bit more as we go forward. It&#8217;s really the story of his family, the family that he creates on Pandora. His extended family. So think of it as a family saga like &#8216;The Godfather.&#8217;&#8221;</p></blockquote>
<p>Plot details on the new <em>Avatar</em> trilogy are tightly under lock and key right now, but we anticipate some variation on the &#8216;settlers vs. natives in space&#8217; narrative of the first movie &#8211; not least of all, because Stephen Lang will reprise as Colonel Quaritch (through <a href="http://screenrant.com/avatar-2-3-4-sequels-cast-stephen-lang-colonel-quaritch/" >means that have yet to be explained</a>), and Cameron has said that the memorable, if two-dimensional, military antagonist from <em>Avatar</em> will <em>&#8220;evolve into really unexpected places&#8221;</em> in the sequels.</p>
<p>_____</p>
<p><strong><em>Avatar 2</em></strong> is slated to open in theaters by December 2016, followed by <strong><em>Avatar 3</em></strong> in December 2017 and <strong><em>Avatar 4</em></strong> in December 2018.</p>
